# Rudolf Ortvay International Competition in Physics  

This directory contains original solutions to selected problems from the **Rudolf Ortvay International Competition in Physics**, conducted annually by Eötvös Loránd University, Budapest, Hungary.

The original competition problems are publicly available on the [official website](https://ortvay.elte.hu/main.html).

The Ortvay competition is distinguished by its depth, conceptual sophistication, and analytical demands. The problems differ in several important respects from many other physics competitions:

- **Graduate-Level Depth**  
  The problems are primarily designed for graduate students in physics and require substantial theoretical maturity and in-depth knowledge across multiple areas of physics.

- **Minimal Guidance**  
  Unlike many structured problem sets, these problems typically do not include intermediate parts or guiding sub-questions. The strategic direction — the *line of thought* — must be identified independently by the solver. Consequently, the initial approach is often nontrivial and may require significant conceptual exploration.

- **Analytical and Computational Demands**  
  Many problems involve extended derivations and demand considerable analytical persistence, technical precision, and computational endurance.
